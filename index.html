The Foundation

These are the core elements you will work with on every page. Mastering them is the first step to creating copy that is both alluring for guests and visible to search engines.

The H1 Tag: The Page Headline

What it is: The single, main title of the page. It's the most important heading and there must be only ONE per page.

Your Task: Craft a compelling H1 that acts as the page's headline and includes the primary topic (keyword) for that page.

Example:

Weak H1:

Welcome

Strong H1:

A 5-Star Luxury Castle Hotel & Spa in the Irish Countryside

Meta Title: The Google Headline

What it is: The blue, clickable headline that appears in a Google search result. It's your digital 'shop window'.

Your Task: Write a unique title for every page, between 30 and 60 characters. It must include the main keyword and your hotel's name.

Practice Tool:

0 / 60

Strong Example:

Award-Winning Luxury Spa & Wellness Retreat | The Gilded Manor

Meta Description: The Google Summary

What it is: The short paragraph of text underneath the blue link in a search result. It entices the user to click.

Your Task: Write a captivating summary of the page, between 80 and 155 characters.

Practice Tool:

0 / 155

Strong Example:

Indulge your senses at our serene wellness spa. Discover bespoke treatments, a thermal suite, and hydrotherapy pools. Book your journey to tranquility.

Open Graph Image: The Social Thumbnail

What it is: The specific image that appears when your page is shared on social media (Facebook, X, LinkedIn). It works with your Meta Title to grab attention.

Your Role: While you don't code this, you should recommend the most compelling, on-brand image for each page. The image is a powerful piece of visual copywriting.

Structuring the Narrative

Once the foundation is set, you must structure the content on the page. This creates a logical flow for the reader and helps search engines understand the hierarchy of your information.

H2 & H3 Tags: The Story's Chapters

What they are: The subheadings of your page. They break up content, making it scannable and easy to digest, like chapters in a book.

Your Task: Use H2 tags for the main sections of your page. Use H3s for finer details under an H2. This creates a clear, logical structure.

Example Structure:

H2: Explore Our Bespoke Suites & Rooms

  • H3: The Presidential Suite
  • H3: Ocean View Junior Suites
  • H3: Classic Manor Rooms

Image Alt Text: Describing the View

What it is: A short, written description of an image. It's vital for accessibility (for screen readers) and helps search engines "see" and index your images.

Your Task: For every important image, write a concise, descriptive phrase. Think: "How would I describe this photo over the phone?"

Example:

Weak Alt Text:

pool.jpg

Strong Alt Text:

Couple relaxing by the infinity pool overlooking the Atlantic Ocean at sunset.

Internal Linking: The Website Concierge

What it is: Simply linking from one page on our website to another relevant page on our website. It guides users and search engines through your site.

Your Task: As you write, when you mention another amenity (like the spa or restaurant), link to that page using descriptive text (called "anchor text").

Visual Example:

Homepage
Dining Page
Spa Page

Links create a web, connecting your content and distributing value.

Mastering the Voice

This is where art meets science. Your tone defines the brand, while understanding user intent and content structure ensures your beautiful words reach the right audience at the right time.

Tone & Style: Our Brand Voice

What it is: The personality and emotion of our brand, expressed through your words. For luxury, this is paramount: elegant, calm, and refined.

Your Task: Emphasize exclusivity, privacy, and comfort. Use emotionally evocative language that appeals to the senses and the desire for escape.

Standard Copy:

The hotel is by the sea. Our hotel is nice and quiet.

Luxury Copy:

A secluded haven nestled along the dramatic Irish coastline. Where time slows, and an aura of elegant tranquility surrounds you.

Search Intent: Understanding the 'Why'

What it is: The underlying goal a person has when they type a query into Google. Are they dreaming, comparing, or ready to book?

Your Task: Tailor your copy to match the user's goal. Match the intent to deliver value and build trust.

Informational ("Dreaming"):

For "best time to visit Ireland," write a helpful guide, not a sales pitch.

Commercial ("Comparing"):

For "luxury spa hotels near Dublin," highlight unique selling points and persuade.

Transactional ("Booking"):

For "book gilded manor suite," make the process clear, direct, and effortless.

Writing for Rich Snippets

What they are: Enhanced, eye-catching search results like star ratings, event details, or FAQ dropdowns. They make our listing stand out.

Your Task: Structure content in a way that Google can easily understand and convert into a rich snippet. A simple FAQ section is a powerful way to do this.

Example FAQ Structure:

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the dress code for the restaurant?

Our restaurant observes a smart casual dress code...

Do you offer valet parking?

Yes, complimentary valet parking is available for all our guests...

The Technical Handshake

These are topics where your writing has a direct impact on the technical side of SEO. You don't need to implement code, but being aware of these concepts helps you avoid common pitfalls.

Duplicate & Thin Content

What it is: Duplicate content is identical text on different pages. Thin content offers little value. Both confuse search engines.

Your Task (CRITICAL): AVOID copy-pasting. Every page, every room description must be unique. Focus on what makes each experience different. Fleshing out every page with rich, descriptive detail avoids thin content.

Canonical URL: The Master Copy

What it is: A technical tag that tells Google, "Of all the pages with similar content, this one is the original."

Your Role: You don't set this tag, but by writing unique content for every page, you prevent the issue from arising. Your distinct copy naturally defines which page is the "master."

URL Structure: The Digital Address

What it is: The web address for the page, e.g., `ourhotel.com/spa/massages`.

Your Role: Your page's H1 title heavily influences the URL. A clear, keyword-focused H1 helps the technical team create a clean, readable URL that both users and search engines can understand.
